Mind-Blowing Jellyfish Facts

Despite their simple biological structure, jellyfish are incredibly complex survivalists.

🧠

No Brain or Heart

Jellyfish are 95% water. They do not have a brain, heart, bones, or lungs. Instead, they navigate using a simple nerve net that detects light, gravity, and chemical changes in the water.

🔄

Biological Immortality

The species Turritopsis dohrnii can reverse its aging process. When damaged or starving, it can revert back to its juvenile polyp stage, starting its life cycle all over again.

🦖

Older than Dinosaurs

Fossil evidence proves that jellyfish have drifted in our seas for more than 500 million years (possibly up to 700 million years), making them far older than dinosaurs and trees.

Stunning Jellyfish Species

Extremely Venomous

Box Jellyfish

Arguably the most venomous marine creature on Earth. Its sting can cause cardiac arrest, paralysis, and death in humans within mere minutes.

Habitat: Indo-Pacific Class: Cubozoa
Gigantic Size

Lion's Mane

One of the largest known species of jellyfish. Its bell can grow up to 2 meters (7 feet) in diameter with tentacles stretching over 36 meters (120 feet) long!

Habitat: Arctic Seas Class: Scyphozoa
Mild Sting

Moon Jellyfish

Recognizable by its translucent bell and four horseshoe-shaped gonads visible in the center. Its sting is incredibly mild and harmless to humans.

Habitat: Worldwide Class: Scyphozoa
Bioluminescent

Crystal Jelly

Famous for its bioluminescence. It is the species from which scientists isolated the Green Fluorescent Protein (GFP), a major breakthrough in medicine.

Habitat: North America Class: Hydrozoa
